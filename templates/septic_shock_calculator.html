About Septic Shock

Septic Shock Definition (Sepsis-3): A subset of sepsis in which underlying circulatory and cellular/metabolic abnormalities are profound enough to substantially increase mortality.

Criteria for Septic Shock:

  • Sepsis present (SOFA ≥2 or clinical suspicion)
  • Persistent hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate volume resuscitation

Mortality: Hospital mortality >40%

Management: Requires immediate intensive care, source control, antibiotics, and hemodynamic support.
